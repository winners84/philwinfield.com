My work explores the relationship between the universality of myth and life as performance. With influences as diverse as Rousseau and Andy Warhol, new variations are created from both explicit and implicit narratives. Ever since I was a postgraduate I have been fascinated by the traditional understanding of the zeitgeist. What starts out as triumph soon becomes manipulated into a carnival of futility, leaving only a sense of unreality and the chance of a new reality. As momentary phenomena become clarified through studious and critical practice, the viewer is left with a glimpse of the undefined of our world.

GDHit (Graphic Design History Interactive Timeline)

The Graphic Design History: Interactive Timeline (GDHit) intends to become a user-generated resource for students, faculty, graphic designers, and just about anyone who is interested in the history of graphic design. The timeline is filtered chronologically, displaying content initially by decade. From this start-point, users can view contributed articles via various categories of graphic design, events of graphic design, tagged words, or through searching the database.

These options allow the user to navigate the history of graphic design in either linear or non-linear formats. Viewers to the timeline are able to contribute knowledgable content of their own by completing the simple "Add Content" form within the timeline toolbar.
